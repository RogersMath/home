How I work

One missing concept can explain everything.

I had a student who was failing Trigonometry — genuinely struggling, falling behind, convinced he just wasn't a math person. When I looked at his textbook and notes, I noticed something: the unit circle was never mentioned. Not once.

"Once I explained the unit circle, everything clicked. Even starting from that difficult place, he still finished with a B."

That story plays out differently every time, but the pattern is almost always the same: there's a concept that was skipped, rushed, or poorly explained — and every lesson since has been built on sand. My job in the first session is to find it.

I've seen this from every angle. As a Finance and Economics graduate, I learned how math serves real-world reasoning. As a Learning Lab Coordinator, I watched how college expects you to think differently than high school did. As a tutor, I've sat with thousands of students at the moment things finally made sense. Those perspectives together let me diagnose faster than most.
